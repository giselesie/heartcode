National Identity






Education


Students from primary to secondary schools sing the national anthem and recite the pledge at the start of every school day.

On 21 July yearly, Racial Harmony Day is celebrated in schools. Students may even choose to dress in traditional costumes such as Cheongsam, Buju Kurung & Sari etc.






SG50


The year 2015 has great significance and all of us come together to celebrate the little things that make us unique Singaporeans. Various SG50 events are held to celebrate this important year.

Exciting events such as Youth Celebrate! and the grand opening of the National Gallery Singapore were held for everyone to enjoy.

Community






Volunteerism


Volunteerism has been imparted in students from a young age. Schools actively encourage students to participate in volunteer activities out of school, such as visiting elderly daycare centres, orphanages etc.

Events such as The Orange Ribbon Run (ORR) helps to rally the community to take a stand against racism and promote graciousness among the races.






Youths

There are many opportunities for youths to connect with each other at different events. Like-minded youths can share their thoughts with one another and mantain the harmony among youths of different races.

Through events like, the SpeakUp! Dialogues Series, youths can take part in discussions about race relations. This encourages youth advocacy and the younger generation can speak their mind freely.
